If you have decided to be a transcriber then it's the time to talk about what you need to be a transcriber.
**********
In order to become a transcriber, the first thing you'll need is a good command of English. (or French, Arabic... for that sake.) Transcription is acquiring video or audio and type it in a perfect way without missing a single word. You'll have to deal with many accents and audios from low quality like telephone calls to audios that are difficult to hear because the speaker is a bit far from the recording device. Some of the audios you'll transcribe also have background noise that'll will make it difficult to understand what the speaker is saying. A good command of the language you're transcribing will help you to understand the words missing due to difficult audio segments.
***********
Next issue is a grammar and punctuation. I hope yoi'll understand by now that the speakers in the audio you're transcribing are not saying "put a comma here!" "here you should put a period" etc... (unless it's a dictation) So punctuation is totally upon you and failing to know where to put punctuation on the transcribed text can really affect your career as a transcriber. Grammar is also supposed to be considered important in transcription world cause of editing a transcription as a full verbatim (Everything as it's said) or clean verbatim (clearing out filler words and false starters and making the transcribed text readable) will mostly depends on it.
************
Once we're done with the skills that're needed to start our career as a transcriber, we must own some materials in order for the business to work. The first one is a desktop/laptop. Although sometimes when you're on the go, you can transcribe on an iPad, tablet or any other smart phone, one of those two items (Desktop/Laptop) is a must have. You'll also need to have a good quality headphone so that you can listen to the audio as clear as possible. There are some softwares which will be either basic or just assistance for the job. (We shall discuss about them one by one in the coming days) And I am not sure if there's one in Kenya but, a foot pedal will also make your work a bit easier.  
************
Now acquiring all the skills and materials doesn't give you a green light to start earning cash right away. You'll need to be trained. (Or you can just decide to train yourself) Anyway you'll have to practice and work on practice files foe quite sometime before you even consider applying in any online company. Because most of them have an entrance exam and the exam is set to those people who at least have a bit experience to start with. So unless you start practicing now, you might not be a transcriber in a near future.

Is Transcribing the right career for you?
**********
Most Kenyans think online business is scam scheme. They will be interested to work at any online job and set themselves to go. But once they start working they won't last on the business so long due to many reasons.
**********
Of all those reasons the main one is accepting the reality that online business is a job as any other job. It has it's way of career building and development to reach at the end point which will be success. Online job will not make you rich instantly. Online job is not like betting with Sportpesa or so...
**********
Transcribing is one of many online jobs that're available for Kenyans to Join. It is a great career if you're interested in it and want to make out of it. But as any online job, it'as it's own pros and cons. We'll cover some of them in this article.
**********
Pros of working for transcription industry-
*
1) The requirement knowledge to be a transcriber is very little. Actually good knowledge of English language and basic knowledge of computer will do.
2) As any of online jobs, Transcription industry is also a work from home scheme. You'll choose your working time, you'll choose your working place (like if you want to do it from your bedroom, at the garden or on the beach) and also you will choose whom to work for as we have many online companies who offer this freelancing job.
3) If you're willing to learn the inside out of the industry and can play by the rule, transcription can be a great source of money for University students, home stay moms, unemployed graduates and even other people who want to be free of time records.
*
Cons of working for transcription industry-
*
1) It's not an instant cash source. It's not as in you need cash for house rent and you come and get it story. It's not your ATM when you start it. It needs discipline, dedication and goals to look upwards in order to become your cash source. You can't come today and expect to get paid a load of cash next week.
2) It requires you to be very attentive to guidelines that the company your work for provide. You need also to have a good listening skill and that requires a calm working environment. You might have to glue yourself on the computer for long hours so you'll have to consider exercising every now and then in order to avoid the fatigue you're going to feel. You need to be very clever to survive in the first rookie time so that you can avoid packing your things and go home within the first two weeks. (Yeah you heard that right! Online companies also fire people!)
3) Your job needs hi speed internet. I know some people work with data bundles, but with a lot of terms, unfamiliar name of places or people and so on to research, u need to be connected to the internet to do the job. Remember you're downloading audios that need to be transcribed and after all the job is called online job for a reason.
